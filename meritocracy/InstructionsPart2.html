Thank you for your effort. We will now start Part 2.

First, the computer will randomly select either you or your paired participant. Each of you is equally likely to be selected.

Your earnings from this part are determined as follows:

  • With some unknown chance p, the computer will intervene and declare the selected person the tournament winner, regardless of their performance in Part 1. The winner receives a bonus of {{ session.config.prize }}.
  • With the remaining chance (1-p), the computer does not intervene. In this case, the outcome is determined exactly as in Part 1: the person who solved more puzzles in Part 1 wins again and receives {{ session.config.prize }}. In the case of a tie, the winner is randomly determined.

Examples

  • If p is 20%, then in 20 out of 100 cases the computer intervenes, and the randomly selected person by the computer wins regardless of performance. In the remaining 80 out of 100 cases, the computer does not intervene, and the winner is determined by performance in Part 1.
  • If p is 75%, then in 75 out of 100 cases the computer intervenes, and the randomly selected person by the computer wins regardless of performance. In the remaining 25 out of 100 cases, the winner is determined by performance in Part 1.
